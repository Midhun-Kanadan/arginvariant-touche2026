ANNOTATION GUIDELINE:
A tweet is an ARGUMENT if it contains at least one of:

(A) MAJOR_CLAIM — the author's overall thesis or stance:
  - Explicitly signaled by opinion expressions: "I think", "I believe", "I strongly feel", "In my opinion", "I agree/disagree with", "I am convinced that"
  - May appear without an opinion marker if clearly the author's central evaluative assertion
  - Implicit major claims count: if the author's stance is clearly implied even without explicit statement
  - Rhetorical question implying a position (e.g. "Why should X get to decide Y?" implies X should not)

(B) CLAIM — a direct reason for or against the major claim:
  - Contestable proposition that itself needs support
  - Signaled by forward discourse markers: "therefore", "thus", "hence", "consequently", "as a result", "so" used to draw a positional conclusion
  - A sub-assertion the author is defending, not merely reporting

(C) PREMISE — a reason/justification supporting or attacking a claim:
  - Signaled by backward discourse markers: "because", "since", "for example", "for instance", "furthermore", "in addition", "given that", "due to"
  - Evidence, example, statistic, or personal experience cited to support a claim

ARGUMENT rules:
- ANY ONE of Major Claim, Claim, or Premise makes the tweet an ARGUMENT
- Multi-sentence tweet: at least one claim anywhere = ARGUMENT
- Implicit claims count — inference from context is valid
- Insults are NOT claims and do not make a tweet an ARGUMENT

NOT-ARGUMENT: a tweet that contains NONE of the above because:
  - It only reports facts or relays information neutrally
  - It asks a genuine (non-rhetorical) question
  - It contains only insults without a reasoned proposition
  - It only makes future recommendations without a positional claim as basis
  - It uses shell language announcing reasons without stating them ("There are many reasons why...")
  - It requires a missing parent tweet to be interpretable
  - It is written in a language other than English

CRITICAL RULES:
1. Implicit claims are valid — do not require explicit markers.
2. Insults (e.g. "don't be a dick") are NOT argument components.
3. If the tweet lacks necessary context (references a missing parent tweet), classify as NOT-ARGUMENT.
4. Shell language ("Another reason is that...", "I have many reasons...") is NOT an argument component.
