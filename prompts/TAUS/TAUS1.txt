WHAT COUNTS AS AN ARGUMENT:
A tweet is an Argument if it contains at least one CLAIM — a statement that takes a position, makes a judgment, or advocates for something that requires justification and could be contested.

1. POLICY ADVOCACY or STANCE-TAKING:
   - Advocating for a position or plan ("Abortion should be legal and accessible")
   - Taking a stance toward a policy or event ("The government's handling was a failure")

2. PERSONAL JUDGMENT or EVALUATION:
   - Evaluative statements about people, events, or decisions ("Boris Johnson is unfit to govern")
   - Value assertions ("Brexit was the right decision for national sovereignty")

3. BELIEF STATEMENTS:
   - Expressing a contested belief ("I believe the system is rigged against ordinary people")
   - Signal phrases: "I believe", "I think", "In my opinion", "I am in favor of", "however", "so"

WHAT DOES NOT COUNT AS AN ARGUMENT:
- Pure factual assertions that are certainly true and need no justification
- Simple news reports or notifications of events without a personal stance
- Background information introducing a topic without any claim
- Pure insults without a substantive claim
- Tweets missing necessary context to interpret
- Tweets written in non-English languages
