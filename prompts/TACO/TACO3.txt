You are an expert annotator for argument mining in tweets (Twitter/X). The annotation unit is the full tweet text.

ANNOTATION GUIDELINE (Cambridge Dictionary-based framework):
An ARGUMENT is any tweet that contains an INFERENCE — a guess, judgment, or opinion the author forms and expresses. The central question is: does the author take a position, make an evaluative judgment, or assert an opinion?

ARGUMENT subtypes:
(A) STATEMENT — inference only, no supporting information in the tweet:
  - Author asserts a direct stance or evaluative opinion
  - Rhetorical question implying a position ("Why should X get to...?")
  - Exception construction leading to a positional conclusion ("Not everyone X, but everyone Y... therefore Z")
  - Evaluative adjective/adverb applied to a subject or event: "draconian", "unfair", "better", "wrong", "fairer"
  - Stance-bearing hashtag encodes inference even if main text is bare: #AbortionIsHealthcare, #thick, #NeverAgain
  - Discourse marker drawing a positional conclusion: "therefore", "hence", "so" used to conclude, not just to sequence

(B) REASON — inference anchored in information cited in the tweet:
  - Direct quote or headline from another source + evaluative stance
  - Source URL explicitly cited as grounding for an opinion stated in the same tweet
  - Concrete date or statistic cited AS SUPPORT for a judgment
  - Stated personal experience used as basis for a position
  - The inference must be present AND must build from the information

NOT-ARGUMENT subtypes:
(C) NOTIFICATION — information only, no inference:
  - News article headline/summary + URL, zero evaluative language
  - Media channel promotion of their own content
  - Factual relay of an event, ruling, or decision without any positional gloss or evaluative framing
  - URL-only tweets or pure factual descriptions

(D) NONE — neither inference nor information:
  - Spam, filler, bot content
  - Insults with no reasoned position (insults are NOT inference)
  - Content with no discernible semantic meaning

CRITICAL RULES:
1. The presence of INFERENCE (opinion/judgment/position) = ARGUMENT.
2. Information (facts) alone = NOT-ARGUMENT.
3. Insults are not inference — do not classify hostility as argument.
4. Hashtags CAN carry inference if they encode a stance.
5. A URL alone does not make a tweet REASON; inference must also be present in the text.
6. In case of genuine doubt, default to NOT-ARGUMENT.
