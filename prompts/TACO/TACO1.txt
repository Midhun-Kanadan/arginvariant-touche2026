DATASET CONTEXT: Tweets from Twitter/X debates on political and social topics (abortion, Brexit, Squid Game, etc.).

TASK: Classify each tweet as "Argument" or "No-Argument" using the Inference-Information framework.

WHAT COUNTS AS AN ARGUMENT:
A tweet is an Argument if it contains an INFERENCE — a guess, opinion, or judgment the author forms, possibly based on information in the tweet. Two types:

1. STATEMENT — Inference only (no supporting information stated):
   - A standpoint or assertion without cited evidence ("This policy is wrong")
   - A rhetorical question implying a position ("How can anyone defend that?")
   - A hashtag expressing a viewpoint (#AbortionIsHealthcare, #Brexit)

2. REASON — Inference based on Information (both present):
   - An opinion backed by a fact, quote, or source-reference in the tweet
   - A conclusion drawn from stated evidence

KEY TEST: Ask "What does the author want me to BELIEVE?" If you can identify a belief the author is trying to convey → Argument.

WHAT DOES NOT COUNT AS AN ARGUMENT:
1. NOTIFICATION — Information only, no inference:
   - Sharing or summarising a news article without adding an opinion
   - Reporting a factual event ("Mexico's Supreme Court ruled X unconstitutional")

2. NONE — Neither inference nor information:
   - Spam, gibberish, pure insults, off-topic content
   - Tweets in non-English languages

DECISION RULE: Can you identify what the author wants you to BELIEVE (inference)? If yes → Argument. If the tweet only states facts/news, or contains nothing meaningful → No-Argument. When genuinely unsure, lean toward No-Argument.
